<p><ul><li>Prestonian Type</li><li>Non-Prestonian Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Chemical Mechanical Planarization (CMP) slurries are essential in semiconductor manufacturing, facilitating the polishing of wafer surfaces. The Prestonian type is characterized by a linear relationship between polishing rate and downforce, leading to consistent and predictable results. In contrast, the Non-Prestonian type exhibits a more complex behavior, where the polishing rate may not directly correlate with the applied pressure, often resulting in non-linear responses. Both types cater to different polishing requirements, influencing performance and efficiency in the CMP process.</p></p>
