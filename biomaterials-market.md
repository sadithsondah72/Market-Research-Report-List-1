<p><p>The biomaterials market includes various types: **Metallic Biomaterials** (e.g., titanium, stainless steel) are durable and used in implants. **Ceramic Biomaterials** (e.g., alumina, zirconia) are biocompatible and ideal for dental and bone applications. **Polymeric Biomaterials** (e.g., silicone, polyethylene) are flexible and used in soft tissue replacements. **Natural Biomaterials** (e.g., collagen, chitosan) are derived from nature and promote healing. **Composites** combine materials (e.g., polymer-ceramic) to enhance properties for specific medical needs. Each type caters to unique biomedical applications, driving innovation in healthcare.</p></p>
